The Story

I started making music in 2009 when I was a freshman in high school. Just a kid with a dream and access to a computer. I didn't know where it would go, but I knew I had to do it.

In 2016, I lost my mom. That changed everything. Music went from something I loved to something I needed. It became the way I processed the pain, the way I kept going when everything felt impossible.

I've had some wins—producing for Lil Wayne, building TierOneRecords, putting out my own music. But I'm not gonna lie and say it's been easy. Right now, I'm in federal prison. That's my reality. But even in here, I'm still making music, still building, still pushing forward.

TRIAL AND ERROR means exactly what it sounds like. You try, you fail, you learn, you try again. That's it. No matter what you're going through, you can keep moving. Your circumstances don't have to be your ending. That's what I'm proving to myself every day.

TRIAL AND ERROR is more than just words—it's a lifestyle.
